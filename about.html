Food Insecurity

The majority of the Canadians households are unware what 12% food-insecure households are tackling: some of them have to go vegans when out of pockets, not out of healthy concerns. What’s worse, food insecurity influences the poor, in addition to other groups as students, elders and children. A research exposes that as high as 39% students experienced some degree of food insecurity, for example 86.2% interviewed students will apply for a loan or bursary to cope with food insecurity. However, less than one-quarter of food-insecure households utilize food banks, and barriers can be time, food skills and the availability of processed foods. Moreover, those who use the established food system risk nutritional problems in the long term, as research show.


Food Waste

Meanwhile, there was an estimation that $31 billion worth of food would turn out to be garbage in 2014, equivalent to 2% of Canadian’s gross domestic product (GDP) in 2013, which was enough for the whole Canada for a whole winter 7 . Sorrowfully, the National Geographic points out, “about one third of the planet’s food goes to waste, often because of its looks.” 8 Moreover, the true cost is of the disposal of this $31 million worth trash is astronomical 9 - the cost of labour, energy transportation, inventory, disposal, and financial audits.
